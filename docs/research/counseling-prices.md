# What couples counseling actually costs in the United States

Research compiled 2026-07-19. Every figure below was accessed on 2026-07-19 unless noted.

**Purpose.** Mend makes pricing comparisons in marketing and in the paywall. This document exists so those comparisons are defensible. Only figures in the "Verified figures" table should ever appear in shipped copy, and only with the hedging language attached to each row.

**Scope note.** This app serves all committed romantic relationships. The industry uses "couples therapy," "marriage counseling," and "relationship counseling" interchangeably in pricing pages. Where a source says "marriage counseling," that is the source's word, not ours. Our own copy should say "couples counseling" or "relationship counseling."

---

## Bottom line for the product team

1. There is **no authoritative national average for couples therapy specifically**. Nobody publishes one with real methodology. Anyone quoting a precise national number is guessing. Use a range, always.
2. The defensible in-person range is roughly **$100 to $250 per session**, with the middle of that band being where most out-of-pocket couples land.
3. A typical course is **8 to 20 sessions**, occasionally more. That puts a realistic **total course cost at roughly $1,200 to $5,000** out of pocket.
4. **Insurance usually does not cover it** as relationship work. It can be covered when one partner is the "identified patient" with a mental health diagnosis and the therapy treats that diagnosis. That nuance must survive into any copy we write.
5. The cheapest legitimate paths are **training clinics, community health centers, and Open Path Collective**, roughly **$20 to $90 per session**.
6. The widely circulated "41% of couples cite cost as a barrier" figure is **unverifiable and should never be published**. See the do-not-claim section.

**Critical caveat on the $139.** This is the single best-sourced session price in this document, and it is **not couples therapy**. If we use it at all, label it "the national average for a therapy session across all types."

**Model differences that matter for honest comparison.**

- Talkspace and Regain are **subscriptions**, billed weekly or monthly whether or not you attend. Talkspace's couples plan buys four 30-minute video sessions a month. That is **half the clock time** of a standard 50-minute in-person session, so a raw price-per-session comparison against in-person therapy is misleading.
- Regain buys one live session per week (Regain does not state session length on the page cited), plus messaging, on one subscription for both partners.
- Ours is **per session or per package**, closer to a private-practice model.
- Any comparison table we ship must state session length and session count, not just dollars.

| Copay, second source | $30 to $50 per session | https://growtherapy.com/blog/how-much-does-marriage-counseling-cost/ | 2026-07-19 |

**Plain-language version of the insurance answer, safe to paraphrase.** US health insurance is built around treating a diagnosed condition in one person. Relationship problems are not a diagnosis. So couples counseling is often not covered on its own. It can be covered when one partner has a diagnosed mental health condition and the couples sessions are treating that condition, billed under code 90847 with that partner named as the patient. That means one partner ends up with a mental health diagnosis in their medical record. Coverage varies by plan, so anyone should call their insurer before assuming.

**Do not write "insurance never covers couples therapy."** That is false and a marketing team should not say it. Write "often not covered on its own."

## Not verified / do not claim

Everything in this section failed verification. **None of it may appear in Mend copy, the paywall, the App Store listing, or any marketing asset.**

### Fabricated or unverifiable statistics

1. **"41% of couples cite cost as a barrier (KFF, 2021)."** Appears on worldmetrics.org and similar aggregator sites. I could not find this figure in any KFF publication. KFF's actual published numbers on cost-driven forgone mental health care are 4% to 7% of all adults, and 23% among untreated adults with anxiety or depression. **Do not publish. This looks fabricated.**
2. **"35% of couples are unable to afford therapy due to cost (Pew Research, 2022)."** Same aggregator sites. No matching Pew publication located. **Do not publish. This looks fabricated.**
3. **Any statistic sourced to worldmetrics.org, wifitalents.com, costinsighthub.com, carecostindex.com, costtrends.org, nationalmentalhealthsupport.com, or similar "statistics" and "cost guide" domains.** These are search-engine content farms with no stated methodology, no named authors, and no primary data. Several publish mutually contradictory numbers. Treat every figure on them as unsourced.

### Numbers that circulate but that I could not confirm

4. **"National average for couples therapy is $100 to $250 per session."** The range itself is plausible and roughly matches the two sources I trust, but no organization publishes an audited national average **for couples therapy specifically**. Present as a range with source attribution, never as "the national average."
5. **Rural at $145 versus NYC at $250.** Sourced only to content farms. Directionally, metro rates are higher, but I have no methodologically sound rural-versus-metro couples number. Note that SimplePractice's real state-level data complicates the simple story: the highest state average is **North Dakota at $227**, a rural state, likely reflecting provider scarcity rather than affluence. **Do not claim a specific rural-versus-urban dollar gap.**
6. **Talkspace "starts at $436 per month" and "$65 per additional session."** These appear in search snippets of the Talkspace help center, but that page returned HTTP 403 and I could not load it. They also conflict with Talkspace's own public pricing page, which I did load and which says $109 per week and $69 per additional session. **Use the $109/week figure from talkspace.com/pricing. Do not use $436.**
7. **"Employee Assistance Programs cover 3 to 8 free couples sessions."** Plausible, and EAPs do commonly cover a small number of sessions, but I found no authoritative source for that specific range. EAP benefits vary enormously by employer. **Do not state a number.**
8. **"Regain offers 10% to 40% financial aid discounts."** Regain's own page confirms "financial aid may be available to those who qualify" but states no percentage. **Do not state a percentage.**
9. **Regain session length.** Search results said 45 to 60 minutes; Regain's own page I loaded does not state a length. **Do not claim a session length for Regain.**
10. **"12 to 25 sessions over 4 to 10 months"** as the couples therapy course length. Came from individual therapist practice blogs. Our two better sources say 8 to 20 and 12 to 20. **Use 8 to 20.**
11. **"EFT is typically 8 to 20 sessions (Johnson, 2008)"** and related effect-size claims. The 8 to 20 range is consistent with our other sources, but I could not read the primary research (the Wiebe and Johnson review PDF would not parse, and I did not obtain the meta-analysis full text). **Do not cite research literature or effect sizes. Cite the practice sources instead.**
12. **University training clinic pricing of "$20 to $60 per session."** Directionally right and consistent with the Council for Relationships figure, but the $20 to $60 range itself came from a therapist blog. Training clinic pricing is genuinely local. **Say "often well under $100 per session, varies by clinic" rather than a national range.**
13. **BetterHelp insurance acceptance as of January 2026 (Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, Aetna, Optum).** From a third-party review site, not confirmed on BetterHelp's own pages. Also irrelevant to couples, since BetterHelp does not offer couples therapy. **Do not claim.**

### Framing errors to avoid

14. **Never say "insurance doesn't cover couples therapy."** Say "often isn't covered on its own." The accurate version is in the insurance section above.
15. **Never present the SimplePractice $139 as a couples therapy figure.** It is individual psychotherapy codes only.
16. **Never compare a Mend subscription price directly against a per-session therapy price without stating what each buys.** Mend is not therapy, does not replace therapy, and does not include a licensed clinician. Any price comparison that implies otherwise is both dishonest and a regulatory risk.

## Safety and positioning guardrails for anyone using this document

These are not optional and do not change based on what the pricing says.

- Mend is an educational and practice tool. It is not therapy, not a substitute for therapy, and not delivered by licensed clinicians. Pricing comparisons must never blur that line. The most a comparison may say is that professional counseling is expensive and that Mend is a low-cost way to practice relationship skills between sessions or before starting.
- Cost framing must never discourage anyone from seeking professional help. If we mention that therapy is expensive, we should mention the low-cost paths in the same breath: Open Path Collective, community health centers, training clinics, and the SAMHSA helpline at 1-800-662-4357.
- Nothing in a pricing or upsell flow may sit in front of, delay, or obscure crisis resources, the domestic violence gate, or per-track red-flag warnings. Safety content stays free and stays first.
- Where anyone is in danger, cost is irrelevant. The National Domestic Violence Hotline is 1-800-799-7233 and the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline is 988. These are free.

## Recommended safe public claims

If marketing needs one sentence, use one of these. Each is backed by the table above.

**Safest, single strongest claim:**

> A course of in-person couples counseling typically runs eight to twenty sessions, which comes to roughly $1,200 to $5,000 out of pocket.
> Sources: Thriveworks, "How Much Is Couples Therapy," published Nov 6 2025 (https://thriveworks.com/help-with/beginning-therapy/how-much-is-couples-therapy/), which gives $150 to $250 per session and eight to twenty sessions; and Grow Therapy, updated Jun 24 2026 (https://growtherapy.com/blog/how-much-does-marriage-counseling-cost/), which gives $75 to $250 per session and $1,200 to $2,000 for a typical course.

**Per-session claim:**

> In-person couples counseling commonly costs $150 to $250 per session out of pocket.
> Source: Thriveworks, published Nov 6 2025.

**Insurance claim:**

> Health insurance often does not cover couples counseling on its own, because plans pay to treat a diagnosed condition in one person and relationship problems are not a diagnosis. It can be covered when one partner has a mental health diagnosis and the sessions are treating it.
> Source: SimplePractice, "Couples and family therapy CPT codes for billing" (https://www.simplepractice.com/blog/billing-couples-family-therapy/).

**Low-cost alternatives claim:**

> Lower-cost counseling exists. Open Path Collective lists couples sessions at $40 to $80 after a one-time $65 membership, and nonprofit training clinics such as Council for Relationships offer intern-led sessions from $20 per hour.
> Sources: https://openpathcollective.org/pricing-and-eligibility-for-affordable-therapy/ and https://councilforrelationships.org/fees. Re-verify the Open Path numbers in a browser first, per the note above.

**Online subscription claim:**

> Subscription couples therapy runs about $109 per week at Talkspace, which includes up to four 30-minute video sessions a month plus messaging, and $70 to $100 per week at Regain, which includes one live session a week plus messaging on a single subscription for both partners.
> Sources: https://www.talkspace.com/pricing and https://www.regain.us/advice/therapist/how-much-does-regain-cost/ (page updated Nov 5 2025).
